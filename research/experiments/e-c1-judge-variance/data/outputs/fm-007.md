# Financial Model: HealthBridge — Series A HealthTech SaaS
## Quality: Borderline (Missing cash flow statement, good narrative)

### Executive Summary

HealthBridge provides HIPAA-compliant patient engagement software for mid-size healthcare practices (50-200 providers). Current ARR is $2.4M with 82 customers. We are projecting growth to $7.8M ARR by Year 3 based on expanding within existing accounts (115% NRR) and new logo acquisition via channel partnerships with EHR vendors.

### Assumptions

| Assumption | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Starting ARR | $2.4M | Actual, Dec 2025 |
| New logo growth | 25 → 35 → 45 customers/year | Channel partner pipeline (signed LOIs with 3 EHR vendors) |
| ACV | $24K (current avg), growing to $30K by Y3 | Upsell of telehealth module ($6K add-on, 40% attach rate by Y3) |
| NRR | 115% | Trailing 12-month; conservative vs. 122% in recent quarters |
| Gross Margin | 75% → 78% | Improving via automation of implementation services |
| Logo Churn | 8% annual | Industry benchmark for healthcare SaaS (Gainsight 2025) |
| Headcount | 30 → 42 → 55 → 68 | Implementation-heavy model requires CS/onboarding staff |
| Avg cost/employee | $140K fully loaded | Healthcare SaaS premium for compliance expertise |
| Sales cycle | 60-90 days | Typical for healthcare IT purchases |

**Why these assumptions are defensible:**
- 25 new customers/year = ~2 per month = realistic for a 4-person sales team with channel support
- 115% NRR is conservative: telehealth module adds $6K/year per customer, and 40% attach by Y3 = $2.4K avg uplift on $24K ACV = 110% from expansion alone
- 8% logo churn reflects the stickiness of healthcare IT (high switching costs, regulatory burden)
